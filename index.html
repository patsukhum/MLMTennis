Motivation

Our research project examines the relationship between a professional male tennis player’s age and rank. Like many professional sports, success in tennis requires early and sustained dedication. Rarely does a tennis player, early in his career, rocket up the rankings without a track record of steady improvements. Typically, it is a gradual process, with a player improving his rank each year until he reaches his peak. Once a player surpasses his physical prime however, his rank will typically decrease, incrementally each year as he ages.

Research Questions

RQ 1: How do a player’s age and home continent predict ranking growth?

Why would a player’s continent matter? Tennis’s popularity varies significantly by country and, by extension, by continent. If tennis is popular in a given region, this usually means there is an institutional body (such as the USTA in the United States) that provides player development, support, and financing. A player from a country and continent where tennis is popular is also more likely to have support from fans.

RQ 2: How do a player’s age and average match performance – e.g. the proportion of serve points won per match, the proportion of breakpoints saved per match–predict his ranking growth?

This question is perhaps more intuitive. We would expect a player with better average match performance statistics to ascend to higher rankings. But what average match statistics are particularly important, and how much difference in ranking, after considering age, do they predict?
